Why this matters
Education was built for one brain type. Yours is different.

Most learning platforms push the same pace, same layout, and same instructions to every student, quietly assuming a neurotypical attention span and processing style.

Standardised content → varied brains
One‑size‑fits‑all → one‑size‑fits‑none
We flip the default to “neurodivergent‑first”
Problem
Invisible cognitive overload

Students with ADHD or high anxiety often hit overload long before the system notices. The app keeps pushing content; the learner silently drops out.

Problem
Dyslexia‑unfriendly interfaces

Dense text, low contrast, and tiny fonts make reading a battle. Conventional platforms rarely adapt typography, spacing, or modality to dyslexic users.

Shift
AI that shapes itself to you

Instead of telling neurodivergent learners to “try harder,” Cognify rewrites how content is chunked, timed, voiced, and displayed based on how their brain actually works.

Inside the system
Four adaptive layers for real learners, not ideal ones.

Under the hood, Cognify combines attention signals, interaction patterns, and self‑reported data to continuously tune the experience: what you see, how long you see it, and when you get to rest.

Layer 01
Cognitive load controller

Tracks micro‑signals like rapid tab‑switching, slowed responses, and error spikes to estimate current load, then shrinks, pauses, or reorders tasks before burnout hits.

Layer 02
ADHD‑aware pacing

Uses short timers, visible progress bars, and micro‑deadlines instead of long, vague assignments. Sessions auto‑split into sprints with built‑in breaks and rewards.

Layer 03
Dyslexia‑friendly reading

Toggles to high‑contrast themes, larger letterspacing, and text‑to‑speech by default for dyslexic profiles, transforming dense paragraphs into guided, multimodal chunks.

Learner profiles
One engine, many brains.

Every learner builds a profile over time. Cognify doesn’t label you; it listens and adapts, so your “ideal way to learn” becomes the default, not the exception.

Profile A
ADHD sprint learner

Short, high‑focus bursts with strong visual cues. Tasks arrive as checklists, not essays. The system auto‑adds timers, breaks, and micro‑rewards to keep momentum.

Profile B
Dyslexic audio‑first learner

Lessons start in audio or video, with optional text overlays. When text appears, it is spacious, high‑contrast, and always paired with narration and highlight tracking.

Profile C
Slow‑pace deep thinker

The engine lengthens deadlines, reduces notifications, and turns fast quizzes into reflective prompts, matching the pace of learners who need extra time to process.
